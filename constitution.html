Preamble & Institutional Charter

We, the students of Namungoona High School, commit ourselves to discipline, respect, responsibility, and academic excellence. These articles guide conduct, and any violation shall attract corrective disciplinary action aimed at building character.

Our Mission

To provide quality education in a disciplined environment, nurturing responsible citizens who strive to excel and contribute positively to society.

Our Vision

To be a centre of academic excellence and character development, recognized for shaping future leaders with integrity, innovation, and purpose.

Our Discipline Code (Core Values & Rules)

  • We respect ourselves, others, and school property.
  • We are punctual, prepared, and committed to learning.
  • We Uphold honesty, integrity, and responsibility in all we do.
  • We maintain a clean, safe, and orderly environment.
  • We follow school rules and accept consequences.
  • We reject corruption, bullying, and all forms of misconduct.
  • We wear our uniform with pride and act as ambassadors of the school.

CHAPTER ONE: GENERAL PRINCIPLES

Article 1: Core Values Expansion

  • Discipline: Ensures students follow rules, manage behavior, and remain focused on goals, creating an environment where learning thrives and order is maintained consistently across all school activities.
  • Respect: Promotes positive relationships among students, teachers, and leaders, encouraging understanding, tolerance, and proper conduct, which strengthens unity and harmony within the school community.
  • Integrity: Requires honesty and moral uprightness, ensuring students act truthfully, avoid wrongdoing, and uphold ethical standards in academics, relationships, and all school responsibilities.
  • Responsibility: Ensures students are accountable for their actions, duties, and decisions, promoting maturity, independence, and commitment to both academic work and personal behavior expectations.
  • Justice: Ensures fairness in all decisions, guaranteeing equal treatment, unbiased judgment, and proper handling of cases, allowing every student to receive what is rightfully deserved.

Article 2: Constitutional Objectives

  • Maintain Order and Discipline: This objective ensures the school environment remains organized, controlled, and conducive for learning, preventing chaos, promoting good behavior, and supporting smooth running of daily activities.
  • Ensure Fairness in Handling Cases: Fairness guarantees that all students are treated equally during disciplinary processes, ensuring decisions are based strictly on facts and evidence rather than bias or personal influence.
  • Promote Accountability Among Students: This objective encourages students to take responsibility for their actions, accept consequences, and develop self-discipline, preparing them to become responsible individuals in society.
  • Support Academic Excellence: The Constitution promotes a focused learning environment where students are encouraged to work hard, remain disciplined, and achieve high academic standards consistently.

CHAPTER TWO: STRUCTURE OF THE SCHOOL COURT

To systematically manage discipline, the school institutes a formal judicial branch consisting of specialized desks:

  • Chief Judge
  • Deputy Chief Judge
  • Prosecutor / Attorney General
  • Court Clerk
  • Deputy Court Clerk
  • The Intelligence Desk System
  • Advocates / Defense Lawyers
  • Court Spokesperson

CHAPTER THREE: DETAILED ROLES OF COURT OFFICIALS

Article 3: The Chief Judge

Presides over all court sessions, reviews complex case files, maintains strict order within hearings, delivers final fair judgments based on facts, and oversees the overall execution of duties by other court officials. Furthermore, the Chief Judge:

  • Interprets school laws and regulations to ensure decisions made are consistent, fair, and aligned with established legal principles.
  • Supervises all court officials, ensuring duties are performed responsibly and efficiently for smooth functioning of the entire court system.
  • Maintains discipline in the courtroom, ensuring respect, silence, and proper behavior among all participants during hearings always.
  • Reviews case files before hearings to understand details, identify key issues, and prepare for fair and informed judgments.
  • Handles appeals and complex cases requiring deeper understanding, ensuring justice is achieved even in sensitive or difficult situations.
  • Represents the court in official school matters, promoting its integrity and maintaining good relations with administration and students.
  • Ensures confidentiality of sensitive information, protecting individuals involved in cases and maintaining trust in the court system.
  • Mentors junior members, guiding them in legal procedures, ethics, and responsibilities to strengthen the effectiveness of the court.

Article 4: The Deputy Chief Judge

Assists the Chief Judge in organizing legal files, supervises the general professional conduct of court members, and steps in to preside over sessions when the Chief Judge is unavailable. Furthermore, the Deputy Chief Judge:

  • Acts as a primary link between Chief Judge and officials, ensuring instructions are clearly communicated and properly implemented.

Article 5: The Prosecutor / Attorney General

The Prosecutor represents the school in legal cases, presenting charges against accused individuals and ensuring justice through proper legal procedures. Key roles include:

  • Investigates reported offenses, gathering facts and evidence necessary to build strong and credible cases for court proceedings.
  • Collects and organizes evidence, ensuring all information presented in court is accurate, relevant, and supports the case effectively.
  • Presents arguments in court, clearly explaining charges and evidence to convince the judge of the accused person's responsibility.
  • Questions witnesses during hearings, extracting important information that strengthens the case and clarifies facts surrounding the alleged offense.
  • Works closely with the Intelligence Desk, ensuring investigations are thorough and evidence collected is reliable and well documented.
  • Prepares legal documents, including charges and case summaries required for effective presentation and handling of cases in court.

Article 6: The Court Clerk & Deputy Court Clerk

The Court Clerk coordinates records, manages safety structures for court archives, and tracks timeline metrics. Specifically, the roles are broken down as follows:

Court Clerk Framework:

  • Handles official judicial correspondence, ensuring communication between court and involved parties is clear, timely, and properly documented.
  • Ensures absolute accuracy of records, checking details carefully to avoid errors that may affect court decisions or documentation.
  • Maintains confidentiality of sensitive case files, protecting privacy of individuals and ensuring trust in record keeping systems.

Deputy Court Clerk Framework:

  • Assists the Clerk in recording proceedings, ensuring all details are captured accurately during hearings and court sessions.
  • Organizes and files documents, ensuring case materials are properly arranged and easily accessible when required.
  • Prepares materials for hearings, ensuring files and evidence are ready before court sessions begin daily.
  • Steps in when Clerk is absent, ensuring continuity in record keeping and administrative duties without interruption.
  • Helps manage schedules, ensuring cases are arranged properly and parties informed of hearing dates.
  • Notifies involved parties about hearings, ensuring attendance and readiness for court proceedings at all times.

Article 7: The Intelligence Desk

Conducts objective investigations, interviews witnesses regarding incidents, compiles comprehensive investigation findings, and provides early warnings to mitigate behavioral bugs. Key roles include:

  • Monitors student behavior continuously, identifying potential issues early and providing information that may prevent misconduct within school.
  • Verifies information collected from multiple streams, ensuring accuracy and avoiding false details that could affect court decisions unfairly.
  • Maintains confidentiality of sources, protecting individuals who provide information and ensuring trust in the investigation process.
  • Provides early warnings about impending misconduct, helping school address issues before they escalate into serious problems.
  • Avoids bias during investigations, ensuring fairness and objectivity in all information gathered and presented to court.

Article 8: Advocates / Defense Lawyers

Represent accused individuals in court, ensuring their rights are protected and they receive fair treatment throughout legal processes. Key roles include:

  • Prepares rigorous defense arguments, analyzing evidence and developing strategies to present strong cases supporting their clients effectively.
  • Presents evidence in court, ensuring relevant information supporting their client’s innocence is clearly explained to the judge.
  • Cross-examines witnesses, questioning statements to identify inconsistencies and weaken the case presented against their clients.
  • Advises clients on their constitutional legal options, helping them understand procedures and rights during hearings.

Article 9: The Court Spokesperson

Manages public relations for the judicial branch, communicates structural court updates to the student population, and clarifies ongoing legal rules while preserving confidentiality. Key roles include:

  • Protects confidential information, ensuring sensitive details are not disclosed while maintaining necessary communication channels with the public.
  • Prepares public reports and summaries, providing clear and organized information about court activities and decisions.
  • Responds to questions from students directly, clarifying court processes and addressing concerns raised by the school community.
  • Builds structural trust between the court system and students, ensuring openness and reliability in communication about legal matters.
  • Educates students about school rules and justice frameworks, promoting systemic awareness and encouraging responsible behavior within the school community.

CHAPTER FOUR: RIGHTS OF STUDENTS

Article 10: Foundational Constitutional Rights

Every single student under this legal jurisdiction has the right to access and demand the following protections:

  • Right to a Fair Hearing: No disciplinary sanction may be applied without a formal investigation and evidentiary hearing.
  • Right to Legal Representation: Access to professional representation by an appointed student advocate/lawyer during trials.
  • Right to Respectful Treatment: Absolute protection against abusive language or degrading punishment throughout judicial channels.
  • Right to Education: Unhindered access to academic classes, labs, and assignments, unless suspended via formal decree.
  • Right to Full Defense Metrics: Freedom to present witnesses, cross-examine accusers, and provide documents to clear one's name.

CHAPTER FIVE: DUTIES & RESPONSIBILITIES OF STUDENTS

Article 11: Core Responsibilities

In turn, students carry an active obligation to maintain the standard of the school by executing the following protocols:

  • Obey School Rules: Unconditionally obey all established parameters outlined in this framework.
  • Respect Authority Figures: Demonstrate absolute respect to teachers, administrative staff, and student leadership structures.
  • Consistent Academic Attendance: Attend all scheduled classes, preps, assemblies, and official institutional activities.
  • Maintain Core Discipline: Act responsibly and avoid behaviors that distract the community or cause public nuisance.
  • Institutional Ambassadors: Always act as positive ambassadors for the school, protecting its name both inside and outside the campus.

CHAPTER SIX: PROHIBITED BEHAVIORS & SANCTIONS

The code strictly outlines definitions of offenses alongside structured disciplinary actions. Violation of these rules may result in immediate suspension or dismissal from school.

Section A: Prohibited Behaviors

  • Drug & Substance Abuse: Students shall not use or possess alcohol, cigarettes, or narcotic drugs.
    Punishment: Immediate suspension (1–2 weeks), mandatory counseling, and strict behavioral monitoring.
  • Theft: Stealing personal, student, or institutional properties.
    Punishment: Restitution (immediate replacement or payout), suspension, and a full court hearing registry file.
  • Violence & Bullying: Physical fighting, assaults, or the use of vulgar language, insults, or vernacular on campus.
    Punishment: Suspension, writing formal apology letters, and school community manual work allocations.
  • Unauthorized Electronics: Possession of phones, radios, musical instruments, MP3 players, etc.
    Punishment: Immediate confiscation of items; electronics are released only directly to parents/guardians.
  • Gambling: Engaging in card betting, cash gaming, or risk wagering on campus.
    Punishment: Confiscation of stakes, warning letters, and manual labor.
  • Missing Classes: Deliberate absenteeism or dodging classes without written permission from authority.
    Punishment: Extra remedial lessons and academic penalties tracked on report cards.
  • Noise Making: Shouting, whistling, or making any form of noise that disturbs the freedom and concentration of others.
    Punishment: Detention and cleaning duties.
  • Dormitory Trespassing: Girls entering boys’ dormitories or boys entering girls’ dormitories under any circumstance.
    Punishment: Immediate severe suspension and disciplinary probation tracking.
  • Love Affairs: Engaging in romantic or intimate relationships on campus.
    Punishment: Guidance counseling and a parental summons.
  • Unnecessary Movement: Wandering around during scheduled class hours or evening prep blocks.
    Punishment: Verbal warning and loss of break privileges.
  • Visitor Violations: Meeting visitors or picking items on any other day other than the official visitation day.
    Punishment: Immediate suspension of student visitation privileges.
  • Escapism: Sneaking out or leaving the school compound without written permission.
    Punishment: Strict suspension, loss of leave privileges, and monitoring.
  • Cooking by Students: Preparing unauthorized food items within the dormitories or school compound.
    Punishment: Confiscation of all cooking utensils/heaters and a severe warning card.
  • Food Exchange Violations: Bringing or exchanging external foodstuffs outside authorized channels.
    Punishment: Confiscation of items and caution.

Section B: Compulsory Performance Benchmarks

  • Class Attendance: Attendance in all classes is completely mandatory. Failure attracts academic penalty.
  • Academic Assignments: All homework and class work must be completed on time. Failure attracts additional work and teacher follow-up.
  • Exams and Tests: Participation in assessments is compulsory. Dodging results in a zero mark unless justified by a medical form.
  • Prep Attendance: Prep sessions are mandatory for all students. Failure attracts detention.
  • School Functions: Attendance at assemblies, meals, sports, and chapel is required. Failure attracts discipline hours.

Section C: Personal Grooming & Conduct Standards

  • Hair Regulations: Students must maintain acceptable hairstyles that reflect neatness and uniformity. Avoid funny hair styles, French cuts, or long hair beyond half an inch.
  • No Makeup Policy: Students are strictly not allowed to wear makeup, keeping focus on academic work rather than cosmetics.
  • Dress Code Compliance: Students must remain in full school uniform for assembly, classes, outings, hospital visits, and preps. Black flat shoes are mandatory (with socks for boys). Both O and A level students must own two black skirts/trousers and two school T-shirts.
  • Punctuality Metric: Students must report on the exact day and date of the term before 5:00 PM. Punctuality is required at all functions.
  • Sickness Protocols: All forms of sickness must be reported to the school nurse immediately. Students returning from home must provide a verified medical form.
  • Leave Out Regulations: Pass out is obtained ONLY from the Headteacher or Deputy. Students going out must carry identity cards and written pass out chits. On return, they must immediately surrender chits to the issuing authority.

Section D: School Clubs & Societies Framework

  • Society Membership: Any student is free to join any society of his/her own choice to develop talents and leadership.
  • Patron Requirement: No society without an authorized patron (a member of the teaching staff) will be registered or permitted to operate.
  • Communication Protocol: Society leaders must communicate with outside schools or bodies through their patron. Correspondence must be in triplicate, addressed through the Headmaster, and countersigned by the patron.
  • Trip Regulations: No society or club is allowed to make a trip or outing without the physical presence of the teacher patron. Unauthorized trips attract cancellation.

CHAPTER SEVEN: PREFECTORIAL CODE OF CONDUCT

This Leadership Charter defines the rules governing student leaders (prefects), religious leaders, dormitory leaders, and form captains appointed to assist the staff in ensuring efficient administration of the school.

Prefect Body Vision

To establish a disciplined, responsible, united, and visionary student leadership body that inspires excellence, integrity, service, and positive transformation within the school community.

Prefect Body Mission

To promote discipline, cooperation, academic excellence, moral uprightness, responsibility, teamwork, and student welfare through effective leadership, accountability, and exemplary conduct among all students.

Core Values of the Prefectorial Body

All student leaders are elected or appointed strictly on merit and must demonstrate: Discipline, Integrity, Respect, Responsibility, Teamwork, Honesty, Accountability, Leadership by Example, Service to Others, Time Consciousness, Unity, Humility, Hard Work, and Patriotism.

Specific Offices & Portfolios

  • The Head Prefect: Overall student leader and chief representative of the student body. Coordinates all prefects, chairs student leadership meetings, communicates concerns to administration, and oversees general campus order during assemblies and functions.
  • Deputy Head Prefect: Assists the Head Prefect directly in all leadership fields and assumes full authority in their absence. Coordinates communication loops and supervises delegated assignments.
  • Prefectorial Advisor: Guides, mentors, and counsels the prefectural body in executive decision-making, ensuring professional standards and accountability are upheld.
  • Academic Prefect & Deputy: Monitors classroom discipline, promotes academic programs, reading culture, peer-discussions, and checks attendance files.
  • Entertainment Prefect & Deputy: Organizes school entertainment activities, coordinates music, talent shows, and social events, ensuring they reflect school discipline and values.
  • Sports Prefect & Deputy: Coordinates sports activities, matches, equipment management, and encourages sportsmanship, fitness, and order during games.
  • Health Prefect & Deputy: Monitors sanitation standards, cleanliness, personal hygiene, and health awareness. Assists the school nurse in tracking student well-being.
  • Compound Prefect & Deputy: Supervises compound maintenance, cleaning schedules, slash duties, and trash disposal systems across school grounds.
  • Security Prefect & Deputy: Helps maintain peace and safety, monitors student movement, and reports suspicious behaviors or security breeches immediately to administration.
  • Library Prefect & Deputy: Supervises discipline and strict silence within the library, encourages reading culture, and ensures proper handling of books.
  • Chapel Prefect & Deputy: Coordinates spiritual programs, worship sessions, religious gatherings, and encourages moral conduct and respect for beliefs.
  • Dormitory Prefect: Supervises discipline, cleaning regimes, and student welfare within the residential wings, promoting peaceful coexistence.
  • Language Prefect & Deputy: Promotes correct language communication, monitors language rule compliance, and discourages the use of vernacular.
  • Discipline Prefect & Deputy: Assists the staff directly in enforcing school rules, tracking indiscipline records, and discouraging misconduct across the student population.
  • MDD Prefect & Deputy: Coordinates music, dance, and drama activities, rehearsals, and cultural appreciation segments during school events.
  • Clubs and Societies Prefect & Deputy: Coordinates student clubs, monitors participation, and ensures all groups operate actively under verified teacher patrons.
  • Welfare Prefect: Focuses on student well-being, identifies dining or comfort concerns, and relays them to the student leadership meetings.

CHAPTER EIGHT: UGANDA NATIONAL STUDENTS ASSOCIATION (UNSA)

Namungoona High School maintains compliance with national student leadership frameworks. The UNSA branch represents national student integration interests.

UNSA Core Purpose

To represent and protect student interests, promote academic excellence, uphold discipline, ensure inclusivity, and strengthen leadership capacity among students across Uganda. All operations prioritize student welfare, fairness, and peaceful conflict resolution.

Offices of the National Student Association Branch

  • President: Overall head of the association branch, providing strategic direction and representing students at national symposia.
  • Chief Judge: Oversees disciplinary hearings, interprets student rules fairly, and ensures justice and impartiality in handling disputes and cases.
  • Deputy Chief Judge: Assists in judicial processes, hearings, and ensures fairness in disciplinary procedures when the Chief Judge is unavailable.
  • Speaker & Deputy Speaker: Presides over association parliamentary assemblies, maintains order during deliberations, and ensures proper parliamentary procedures are followed.
  • Publicity Secretary: Manages external communication, public relations, announcements, and information dissemination to stakeholders.
  • Welfare Secretary: Oversees student comfort programs, support networks, and initiatives aimed at improving student morale.

General Principle: No member shall engage in corruption, bias, misuse of office, or abuse of authority. Confidential matters discussed in official meetings must remain fully protected.

CHAPTER NINE: JUDICIAL PROCESSES & TRIAL MECHANICS

Article 12: Evidence & Trial Mechanics

Cases require clear factual verification by the Intelligence Desk and Prosecutor before trials can proceed. The following steps govern the court room flow:

  • Evidentiary Packaging: The prosecution presents organized, verified evidence packages to the Court Clerk. Fake or biased data is thrown out.
  • Defense Arguments: Accused students have full access to an advocate who can analyze briefs, call cross-examinations, and question witnesses to reveal structural inconsistencies.
  • Judgment by Chief Judge: The Chief Judge evaluates all evidence and delivers a fair decision based on facts, ensuring justice, discipline, and consistency with school rules and regulations.
  • Appeals Framework: Students may appeal decisions through the school administration channels to ensure fairness, allowing review of cases where errors, misunderstandings, or dissatisfaction with judgment may have occurred.

CHAPTER TEN: COMMUNICATION RULES

To prevent misinformation, confusion, and unfair influence on decisions within the school, the following communication matrix is established:

  • Official Communication: All information regarding judicial branch updates, rules, or alerts must pass cleanly through the court Spokesperson to ensure consistency, accuracy, and proper representation of the court's position.
  • Confidentiality Mandate: Sensitive case file information must be kept strictly confidential to preserve systemic trust, protect the dignity of individuals, and prevent misuse of data within the school environment.
  • Accurate Reporting: All information shared must be correct, verified, and entirely free of fabrications before dissemination to the student population.

CHAPTER ELEVEN: AMENDMENTS & ENFORCEMENT

Amendment Process: Structural changes to this constitution require mutual approval from both the school administration and student leadership to ensure legitimacy, fairness, and proper consideration before implementation.

Enforcement Mandate: All rules must be followed fully at all times, and violations attract structured punishment based on seriousness and repetition, ensuring discipline, fairness, and order within the school environment.
